1. Mechanics

1.1 Motion and Kinematics

Kinematics describes the motion of objects without considering the forces that cause the motion.

Key Equations of Motion:

  • • v = u + at
  • • s = ut + ½at²
  • • v² = u² + 2as

Where: v = final velocity, u = initial velocity, a = acceleration, t = time, s = displacement

Example Problem:

A car accelerates from rest at 2 m/s² for 10 seconds. Find the final velocity and distance traveled.

Solution:

Given: u = 0 m/s, a = 2 m/s², t = 10 s

Final velocity: v = u + at = 0 + 2(10) = 20 m/s

Distance: s = ut + ½at² = 0(10) + ½(2)(10)² = 100 m

1.2 Newton's Laws of Motion

First Law

An object at rest stays at rest, and an object in motion stays in motion unless acted upon by an unbalanced force.

Also known as the Law of Inertia

Second Law

The acceleration of an object is directly proportional to the net force and inversely proportional to its mass.

F = ma

Third Law

For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.

Forces always occur in pairs

1.3 Energy and Work

Types of Energy:

  • • Kinetic Energy: KE = ½mv²
  • • Potential Energy: PE = mgh
  • • Work Done: W = F × d × cos θ
  • • Power: P = W/t = F × v

Conservation of Energy:

Energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transferred from one form to another.

Total Energy = KE + PE = constant

2. Thermodynamics

2.1 Temperature and Heat

Temperature Scales:

  • • Celsius to Kelvin: K = °C + 273.15
  • • Celsius to Fahrenheit: °F = (9/5)°C + 32
  • • Absolute Zero: 0 K = -273.15°C

Heat Transfer:

Conduction

Through direct contact

Convection

Through fluid movement

Radiation

Through electromagnetic waves

2.2 Laws of Thermodynamics

First Law (Conservation of Energy):

ΔU = Q - W

Change in internal energy = Heat added - Work done by system

Second Law (Entropy):

Heat flows naturally from hot to cold objects

Entropy of an isolated system always increases

3. Waves and Sound

3.1 Wave Properties

Wave Equation:

v = fλ

velocity = frequency × wavelength

Wave Types:

  • • Transverse: Perpendicular vibration
  • • Longitudinal: Parallel vibration
  • • Mechanical: Requires medium
  • • Electromagnetic: No medium needed

Wave Properties:

  • • Amplitude: Maximum displacement
  • • Frequency: Waves per second (Hz)
  • • Period: Time for one complete wave
  • • Wavelength: Distance between peaks

3.2 Sound Waves

Sound Properties:

  • • Speed in air: 343 m/s (at 20°C)
  • • Frequency range (human hearing): 20 Hz - 20,000 Hz
  • • Sound intensity measured in decibels (dB)
  • • Doppler effect: frequency change due to relative motion

Doppler Effect Formula:

f' = f × (v ± v_observer) / (v ± v_source)

Use + when approaching, - when receding

4. Electricity

4.1 Electric Charge and Current

Fundamental Laws:

  • • Coulomb's Law: F = kq₁q₂/r²
  • • Current: I = Q/t (charge per unit time)
  • • Ohm's Law: V = IR
  • • Power: P = VI = I²R = V²/R

Circuit Components:

Resistors:

  • • Series: R_total = R₁ + R₂ + R₃
  • • Parallel: 1/R_total = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂ + 1/R₃

Capacitors:

  • • Series: 1/C_total = 1/C₁ + 1/C₂ + 1/C₃
  • • Parallel: C_total = C₁ + C₂ + C₃

4.2 Electric Fields and Potential

Key Concepts:

  • • Electric Field: E = F/q = kQ/r²
  • • Electric Potential: V = kQ/r
  • • Work Done: W = qV
  • • Capacitance: C = Q/V

5. Magnetism

5.1 Magnetic Fields

Magnetic Force:

  • • On moving charge: F = qvB sin θ
  • • On current-carrying wire: F = BIL sin θ
  • • Between parallel wires: F = μ₀I₁I₂L/(2πd)

Electromagnetic Induction:

  • • Faraday's Law: ε = -dΦ/dt
  • • Lenz's Law: Induced current opposes change
  • • Motional EMF: ε = BLv

6. Optics

6.1 Reflection and Refraction

Laws of Reflection:

  • • Angle of incidence = Angle of reflection
  • • Incident ray, reflected ray, and normal lie in same plane
  • • Mirror equation: 1/f = 1/u + 1/v
  • • Magnification: m = v/u = h'/h

Laws of Refraction:

  • • Snell's Law: n₁ sin θ₁ = n₂ sin θ₂
  • • Refractive index: n = c/v
  • • Critical angle: sin θc = n₂/n₁
  • • Total internal reflection occurs when θ > θc

6.2 Lenses and Optical Instruments

Lens Equations:

  • • Lens equation: 1/f = 1/u + 1/v
  • • Power of lens: P = 1/f (in meters)
  • • Lens maker's equation: 1/f = (n-1)(1/R₁ - 1/R₂)

7. Modern Physics

7.1 Atomic Structure

Key Discoveries:

  • • Photoelectric Effect: E = hf - φ
  • • De Broglie Wavelength: λ = h/p
  • • Bohr Model: mvr = nℏ
  • • Energy Levels: E = -13.6/n² eV (for hydrogen)

Nuclear Physics:

  • • Mass-Energy: E = mc²
  • • Radioactive Decay: N = N₀e^(-λt)
  • • Half-life: t₁/₂ = ln(2)/λ
  • • Binding Energy: BE = (Δm)c²
