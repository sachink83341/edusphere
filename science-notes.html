Biology

Cell Structure and Function

The cell is the basic unit of life. All living organisms are composed of one or more cells.

Plant Cell Components

  • • Cell Wall: Rigid outer layer for protection
  • • Cell Membrane: Controls what enters/exits
  • • Nucleus: Control center containing DNA
  • • Chloroplasts: Site of photosynthesis
  • • Vacuole: Large storage compartment
  • • Mitochondria: Powerhouse of the cell

Animal Cell Components

  • • Cell Membrane: Flexible boundary
  • • Nucleus: Contains genetic material
  • • Mitochondria: Energy production
  • • Ribosomes: Protein synthesis
  • • Lysosomes: Digestive organelles
  • • Centrioles: Cell division

Key Processes:

Photosynthesis:

6CO₂ + 6H₂O + light energy → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂

Cellular Respiration:

C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ → 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + ATP

Genetics and Heredity

Genetics is the study of heredity and the variation of inherited characteristics.

Key Terms:

  • • Gene: A unit of heredity; segment of DNA
  • • Allele: Different versions of the same gene
  • • Genotype: Genetic makeup (e.g., AA, Aa, aa)
  • • Phenotype: Observable characteristics
  • • Dominant: Expressed when present (A)
  • • Recessive: Only expressed when homozygous (a)

Mendel's Laws:

  1. 1. Law of Segregation: Each parent contributes one allele for each trait
  2. 2. Law of Independent Assortment: Genes for different traits assort independently
  3. 3. Law of Dominance: Some alleles are dominant over others

Human Body Systems

Circulatory System

  • • Heart: 4-chambered pump
  • • Blood vessels: Arteries, veins, capillaries
  • • Functions: Transport nutrients, oxygen, waste
  • • Blood types: A, B, AB, O

Respiratory System

  • • Lungs: Gas exchange organs
  • • Alveoli: Site of gas exchange
  • • Functions: Oxygen intake, CO₂ removal
  • • Breathing rate: 12-20 breaths/minute

Digestive System

  • • Mouth: Mechanical and chemical breakdown
  • • Stomach: Acid digestion
  • • Small intestine: Nutrient absorption
  • • Large intestine: Water absorption

Nervous System

  • • Brain: Control center
  • • Spinal cord: Message pathway
  • • Neurons: Nerve cells
  • • Functions: Coordination, response

Chemistry

Atomic Structure

An atom is the smallest unit of matter that retains the properties of an element.

Subatomic Particles:

Protons

  • • Positive charge (+1)
  • • Mass: 1 amu
  • • Located in nucleus

Neutrons

  • • No charge (0)
  • • Mass: 1 amu
  • • Located in nucleus

Electrons

  • • Negative charge (-1)
  • • Mass: ~0 amu
  • • Orbit nucleus

Electron Configuration:

Electrons occupy energy levels (shells) around the nucleus:

  • • 1st shell: Maximum 2 electrons
  • • 2nd shell: Maximum 8 electrons
  • • 3rd shell: Maximum 18 electrons
  • • 4th shell: Maximum 32 electrons

Periodic Table

The periodic table organizes elements by atomic number and properties.

Periodic Trends:

Atomic Radius:

Decreases across periods, increases down groups

Ionization Energy:

Increases across periods, decreases down groups

Electronegativity:

Increases across periods, decreases down groups

Metallic Character:

Decreases across periods, increases down groups

Important Element Groups:

  • • Group 1: Alkali metals (very reactive)
  • • Group 2: Alkaline earth metals
  • • Group 17: Halogens (very reactive non-metals)
  • • Group 18: Noble gases (unreactive)

Chemical Bonding

Ionic Bonding

  • • Transfer of electrons
  • • Between metals and non-metals
  • • Forms ions (cations and anions)
  • • Example: NaCl (sodium chloride)
  • • High melting/boiling points
  • • Conduct electricity when dissolved

Covalent Bonding

  • • Sharing of electrons
  • • Between non-metals
  • • Forms molecules
  • • Example: H₂O (water)
  • • Lower melting/boiling points
  • • Generally don't conduct electricity

Environmental Science

Ecosystems and Biodiversity

An ecosystem is a community of living organisms interacting with their physical environment.

Components of Ecosystems:

Biotic Factors:

  • • Producers (plants, algae)
  • • Primary consumers (herbivores)
  • • Secondary consumers (carnivores)
  • • Decomposers (bacteria, fungi)

Abiotic Factors:

  • • Temperature
  • • Sunlight
  • • Water availability
  • • Soil nutrients

Energy Flow in Ecosystems:

Energy flows in one direction through food chains:

Sun → Producers → Primary Consumers → Secondary Consumers → Decomposers

Only about 10% of energy transfers between trophic levels.

Climate Change

Greenhouse Effect:

Natural process that warms Earth's surface:

  1. 1. Solar radiation reaches Earth
  2. 2. Earth's surface absorbs energy and warms up
  3. 3. Earth emits infrared radiation
  4. 4. Greenhouse gases trap some of this radiation
  5. 5. Trapped heat warms the atmosphere

Major Greenhouse Gases:

  • • Carbon Dioxide (CO₂): From burning fossil fuels
  • • Methane (CH₄): From agriculture and landfills
  • • Nitrous Oxide (N₂O): From fertilizers
  • • Fluorinated gases: From industrial processes

Effects of Climate Change:

  • • Rising global temperatures
  • • Melting ice caps and glaciers
  • • Rising sea levels
  • • Extreme weather events
  • • Changes in precipitation patterns
  • • Threats to biodiversity

Laboratory Safety

Safety Rules:

  • • Always wear safety goggles
  • • Tie back long hair
  • • Wear closed-toe shoes
  • • No eating or drinking in lab
  • • Wash hands before and after
  • • Read instructions carefully
  • • Report accidents immediately

Chemical Handling:

  • • Never taste chemicals
  • • Use fume hoods for volatile substances
  • • Add acid to water, never water to acid
  • • Use proper measuring tools
  • • Dispose of waste properly
  • • Know location of safety equipment
