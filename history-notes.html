1. Ancient Civilizations

1.1 Mesopotamian Civilization (3500-539 BCE)

Key Features:

  • • Location: Between Tigris and Euphrates rivers (modern-day Iraq)
  • • Major Cities: Babylon, Ur, Assyria
  • • Achievements: First written language (cuneiform), wheel, calendar
  • • Government: City-states ruled by kings
  • • Religion: Polytheistic with ziggurats as temples

Code of Hammurabi (1754 BCE):

One of the earliest written legal codes with 282 laws covering various aspects of daily life, including trade, family relationships, and criminal justice.

Famous for "an eye for an eye" principle

1.2 Ancient Egypt (3100-30 BCE)

Key Characteristics:

  • • Location: Nile River Valley
  • • Periods: Old Kingdom, Middle Kingdom, New Kingdom
  • • Pharaohs: Divine rulers (Khufu, Hatshepsut, Tutankhamun, Cleopatra)
  • • Achievements: Pyramids, hieroglyphics, mummification, medicine
  • • Religion: Polytheistic with emphasis on afterlife

Pyramids of Giza:

  • • Built during 4th Dynasty (2580-2510 BCE)
  • • Great Pyramid built for Pharaoh Khufu
  • • Originally 146.5 meters tall
  • • One of Seven Wonders of Ancient World

Egyptian Contributions:

  • • 365-day calendar
  • • Advanced medicine and surgery
  • • Paper (papyrus)
  • • Decimal system

1.3 Indus Valley Civilization (3300-1300 BCE)

Notable Features:

  • • Major Cities: Harappa, Mohenjo-daro
  • • Urban Planning: Grid pattern streets, drainage systems
  • • Trade: Extensive trade networks with Mesopotamia
  • • Technology: Standardized weights and measures
  • • Decline: Mysterious disappearance around 1300 BCE

1.4 Ancient Greece (800-146 BCE)

Greek Contributions:

  • • Democracy: Developed in Athens
  • • Philosophy: Socrates, Plato, Aristotle
  • • Science: Mathematics, astronomy, medicine
  • • Arts: Theater, sculpture, architecture
  • • Olympic Games: Started 776 BCE

Alexander the Great (356-323 BCE):

Created one of the largest empires in ancient history, spreading Greek culture from Egypt to India. His conquests led to the Hellenistic period.

1.5 Roman Empire (27 BCE - 476/1453 CE)

Roman Achievements:

  • • Law: Roman legal system basis for modern law
  • • Engineering: Roads, aqueducts, Colosseum
  • • Military: Professional legions
  • • Administration: Efficient governance system
  • • Language: Latin spread throughout empire

2. Medieval Period (500-1500 CE)

2.1 Islamic Golden Age (8th-13th centuries)

Major Achievements:

  • • Science: Algebra, astronomy, medicine
  • • Scholars: Al-Khwarizmi, Ibn Sina (Avicenna), Al-Razi
  • • Preservation: Translated and preserved Greek texts
  • • Architecture: Mosques, palaces, geometric designs
  • • Trade: Connected East and West through trade routes

House of Wisdom (Baghdad):

A major intellectual center during the Abbasid Caliphate where scholars translated works from Greek, Persian, and Indian texts into Arabic.

2.2 Byzantine Empire (330-1453 CE)

Key Features:

  • • Capital: Constantinople (modern Istanbul)
  • • Religion: Eastern Orthodox Christianity
  • • Emperor: Justinian I (527-565 CE) - reconquest attempts
  • • Architecture: Hagia Sophia
  • • End: Fall to Ottoman Turks in 1453

2.3 Medieval Europe

Feudalism:

Social and economic system based on land ownership and personal relationships:

  • • King: Owned all land
  • • Lords: Received land grants (fiefs) from king
  • • Knights: Military service in exchange for land
  • • Peasants/Serfs: Worked the land

The Crusades (1095-1291):

Series of religious wars between Christians and Muslims for control of the Holy Land, leading to cultural exchange but also religious conflict.

2.4 Mongol Empire (1206-1368)

Under Genghis Khan and Successors:

  • • Extent: Largest contiguous empire in history
  • • Trade: Facilitated Silk Road trade
  • • Tolerance: Religious and cultural tolerance
  • • Impact: Connected East and West

3. Renaissance & Reformation (14th-17th centuries)

3.1 The Renaissance (14th-17th centuries)

Key Characteristics:

  • • Humanism: Focus on human potential and achievements
  • • Art: Leonardo da Vinci, Michelangelo, Raphael
  • • Science: Copernicus, Galileo, scientific method
  • • Literature: Shakespeare, Dante, Machiavelli
  • • Exploration: Columbus, da Gama, Magellan

Printing Press (1440):

Johannes Gutenberg's invention revolutionized the spread of knowledge and ideas, making books more accessible and accelerating literacy.

3.2 Protestant Reformation (1517-1648)

Martin Luther's 95 Theses (1517):

Challenged Catholic Church practices, particularly the sale of indulgences, leading to:

  • • Religious division in Europe
  • • Rise of Protestant churches
  • • Counter-Reformation by Catholic Church
  • • Religious wars and conflicts

3.3 Age of Exploration (15th-17th centuries)

Major Explorations:

  • • 1492: Columbus reaches Americas
  • • 1498: Vasco da Gama reaches India
  • • 1519-1522: Magellan's circumnavigation
  • • 1607: First permanent English settlement in America

4. Industrial Revolution (1760-1840)

4.1 First Industrial Revolution

Key Innovations:

  • • Steam Engine: James Watt (1769)
  • • Textile Machinery: Spinning jenny, water frame
  • • Transportation: Railways, steamships
  • • Iron Production: Coke smelting process

Social Changes:

  • • Urbanization and factory system
  • • Rise of working class
  • • Child labor issues
  • • Women's changing roles
  • • Growth of middle class

5. World Wars

5.1 World War I (1914-1918)

Causes:

  • • Imperialism: Competition for colonies
  • • Alliances: Triple Alliance vs Triple Entente
  • • Nationalism: Ethnic tensions in Balkans
  • • Trigger: Assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand

Consequences:

  • • Over 16 million deaths
  • • Fall of Ottoman, German, Austrian, and Russian empires
  • • Treaty of Versailles (1919)
  • • League of Nations established
  • • Economic depression in 1930s

5.2 World War II (1939-1945)

Major Events:

  • • 1939: Germany invades Poland
  • • 1941: Pearl Harbor attack, US enters war
  • • 1941: Germany invades Soviet Union
  • • 1944: D-Day invasion of Normandy
  • • 1945: Atomic bombs on Japan, war ends

Holocaust:

Systematic genocide by Nazi Germany resulting in the murder of six million Jews and millions of others, including Roma, disabled individuals, and political prisoners.

6. Modern History (1945-Present)

6.1 Cold War (1947-1991)

Key Features:

  • • Superpowers: USA vs Soviet Union
  • • Ideological conflict: Capitalism vs Communism
  • • Nuclear arms race: Mutually Assured Destruction
  • • Space race: Moon landing (1969)
  • • Proxy wars: Korea, Vietnam, Afghanistan

End of Cold War:

Fall of Berlin Wall (1989) and dissolution of Soviet Union (1991) marked the end of the Cold War era.

6.2 Decolonization

Independence Movements:

  • • India & Pakistan: 1947 (British Empire)
  • • African Nations: 1950s-1970s
  • • Southeast Asia: Post-WWII
  • • Middle East: Gradual independence

7. South Asian History

7.1 Mughal Empire (1526-1857)

Major Rulers:

  • • Babur (1526-1530): Founder, victory at Panipat
  • • Akbar (1556-1605): Religious tolerance, administrative reforms
  • • Shah Jahan (1628-1658): Builder of Taj Mahal
  • • Aurangzeb (1658-1707): Last great Mughal emperor

Cultural Achievements:

  • • Taj Mahal (UNESCO World Heritage Site)
  • • Indo-Islamic architecture
  • • Miniature paintings
  • • Persian and Urdu literature

7.2 British Colonial Period (1757-1947)

Key Events:

  • • 1757: Battle of Plassey
  • • 1857: First War of Independence
  • • 1885: Indian National Congress founded
  • • 1906: All-India Muslim League formed
  • • 1919: Jallianwala Bagh massacre

Independence Movement:

  • • Mahatma Gandhi: Non-violent resistance
  • • Muhammad Ali Jinnah: Pakistan movement
  • • Quit India Movement (1942)
  • • Partition (1947): India and Pakistan

7.3 Pakistan's Formation (1947)

Two-Nation Theory:

The idea that Hindus and Muslims were separate nations requiring separate states:

  • • Based on religious, cultural, and social differences
  • • Advocated by Muhammad Ali Jinnah
  • • Led to creation of Pakistan in 1947
  • • Resulted in mass migration and communal violence
